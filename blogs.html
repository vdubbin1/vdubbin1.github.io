What is it?

Unlike regular crime, Cyber Crime is often stealth and affects both individuals and businesses alike. As a general rule, cyber crime is committed for either financial gain or with intent to cause disruption to a business, group or network.

Types of Cyber Crime include:

  • Ransomware attacks
  • password decryption
  • BEC (Business Email Compromise)
  • Hacking - including DDoS(distributed denial-of-service) attacks.
  • malware attacks - including viruses, worms, trojans, spyware.
  • cyber fraud - including phishing, spear phishing, fishing and whaling.

What is Nmap and why use it?

Before venturing somewhere new, I have no doubt that most people would use some form of mapping to get there, be that either Sat nav or old school physical maps. Nmap is no different. It helps us get a picture of the landscape we wish to work with by telling us what services are running on on which ports. We call this process ‘port scanning’.

A computer uses ports when establishing connections to manage the data and keep it separate. There are two ports in use per one connection. One would be the randomly selected port being used on your local machine and the other would be the open port listening on the remote server(where you want to communicate to). With a total of 65535 ports available on every computer there are plenty of ports available however, without any kind of port scanning, manually checking these ports would take an age.

Here is a summary of the OSI model

Here I have broken down the OSI model into my own terms to help me understand it and hopefully help others too.

Each level of the OSI model is outlined below. With layer number represents where it sits in the hierarchy(level 7 being the first) and in brackets I’ve stated where the layer gets implemented.

Layer 7 - Application (Software)

The Application layer is the network layer for applications to use to transmit data. Data passed to this layer is then passed down into the next layer in the model, the Presentation layer. In practical use, this would be HTTP(Hypertext Transfer Protocol) for example.

Layer 6 - Presentation (Software)

This layer translates the data into a standardised format that can be understood by the application layer in the receiving computer. In addition, this layer handles any encryption, compression or other transformations of the data. The data is then passed on to the next layer in the process, the Session layer.
